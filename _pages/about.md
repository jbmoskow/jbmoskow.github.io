I am a research scientist working in educational technology with an academic background in neuroscience. I received my MSc and PhD in Neuroscience from Queen's University where I studied how the brain plans and controls movements. I have experience in applying scientific thinking to business problems and am a skilled (mostly self-taught) programmer, comfortable in R, Python, and SQL. I am particularly interested in working on problems relating to educational, healthcare, and wellness technology.

Outside of work, I enjoy hiking, playing ultimate, collecting and playing Magic: the Gathering, and watching the Toronto Raptors.
